Kota Balikpapan

Kalimantan Timur


Balikpapan is a city located in East Kalimantan, Indonesia. Balikpapan is famous for being known as the "Oil City" because since the drilling of the Matilda oil well, it has produced a significant amount of oil of high quality. The drilling of the Matilda oil well took place on February 10, 1897, which is also celebrated as Balikpapan's birthday. Balikpapan is also renowned for receiving the Adipura Kencana award five times. Adipura Kencana itself must receive the Adipura award three times consecutively, indirectly resulting in Balikpapan receiving approximately 15 Adipura awards in a row. Today, Balikpapan is well-known for its proximity to the new capital city of Penajam Paser Utara, the future capital of the country.
